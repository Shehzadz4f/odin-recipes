Chicken Do Pyaza Recipe

Dopiaza meaning "two onions" is the name of two separate dishes, one in the Greater Iran region and one in South Asia. It refers to a family of recipes, typically meat-based, that contain onions as a major ingredient. There are two alternative etymological explanations for its name. Onions are added at two stages of cooking: chopped or ground in the marinade/gravy, and as a garnish/topping, either pickled or crisply fried. The recipe uses onions and meat in a 2:1 ratio. The dish usually contains a meat, usually beef, chicken, lamb, mutton, or shrimp; however, it can also be prepared in a vegetarian style, e.g., with okra.

INGREDIENTS:

For Marination,

  • 750 gms Chicken (curry cut), washed and cleaned
  • 2 tbsp Ginger Garlic paste
  • Salt to taste

For Gravy

  • 3 tbsp Ghee
  • 3 Onions, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp Ginger Garlic paste
  • 1 ½ cup Tomato puree
  • 1 cup Yogurt, beaten
  • 1 tsp Turmeric powder
  • 2 tsp Red chilli powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tsp Garam masala powder
  • 2 Green chillies, slit
  • 1 cup Onion Petals
  • 1 tbsp Kasuri Methi
  • Coriander leaves, for garnish

STEPS:

  1. Take the chicken pieces in a bowl, add ginger garlic paste and salt to it. Mix well and keep it aside.(For marination).
  2. Heat ghee in a kadhai and add onions into it. Fry until it’s golden brown in colour.
  3. Add ginger garlic paste to it and mix. Cook until aromatic.
  4. Pour the tomato puree and cook well. Let it release the oil.
  5. In a bowl add yogurt, turmeric powder and red chilli powder. Mix well.
  6. Once the tomato puree is well cooked, pour the yoghurt mixture and stir well.
  7. Add salt and let the yogurt cook on medium low flame.
  8. Drop in the marinated chicken pieces and mix. Cover with lid and cook.
  9. Once the chicken gets half cooked, add garam masala powder, drop in the onion petals, green chillies and mix again. Cover with a lid, cook further until the chicken becomes nice tender and juicy.
  10. Remove the lid and sprinkle kasuri methi on top. Mix it.
  11. Cook for more 5 minutes and turn off the flame.
  12. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.
